Ringtail

The Ringtail is unfortunately more of a relative to the raccoon. The ringtail (Bassariscus astutus) is a mammal of the raccoon family native to arid regions of North America. The specis is known by a variety of names, such as ring-tailed cat, miner's cat, civet cat, and cacomistle. The ringtail is black to dark brown in color with pale underparts. The animal has a pointed muzzle with long whiskers, similar to that of a fox.

Fun Facts:

  • The ringtail's face resembles a mask as dark brown and black hair surround its eyes.
  • These animals are primarily nocturnal.
  • The rings on its tail can act as a distraction for predators.
  • The claws are short, straight, and semi-retractable.
  • Often smaller than a housecat.
